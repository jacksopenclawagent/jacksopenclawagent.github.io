👤 Who is José?

Born in the backstreets of Seville to a flamenco dancer mother and a fisherman father, José Ramirez grew up with music in his bones and salt air in his lungs. By age twelve he was busking on the Triana bridge. By twenty he had a record deal. By twenty-five he walked away from it to open a tapas bar.

Today, at 38, he does both — releasing albums from his kitchen, filming cooking videos between guitar sessions, and living a life that refuses to be put in a single box. "I never understood why people had to choose," he says. "Food and music are the same thing. They both make people feel something."

📅 Life in Chapters

1986

Born in Triana, Seville

Youngest of four children. His mother, a flamenco dancer, put a guitar in his hands at age six.

2004

Moves to Madrid, signs first record deal

At 18, signs with a small independent label. Spends three years playing every venue in the city.

2011

Releases "Sangre y Sal"

Debut album goes gold in Spain and Portugal. Tours Europe for two years straight.

2015

Opens Casa Ramirez, Seville

Walks away from a major label deal to open a 22-seat tapas restaurant in his home neighbourhood.

2019

Year in Tokyo

A residency that changed everything. Studies Japanese fermentation techniques. Returns with a new sound and a second restaurant concept.

2023

"Noche Sin Fin" — album of the year

His most personal record yet. Recorded entirely in his kitchen at 3am, between restaurant shifts.
